Title: Solar System Basics

Q: What star is at the center of our solar system? | A: The Sun
Q: Which planet is known as the Red Planet? | A: Mars
Q: Which planet is closest to the Sun? | A: Mercury
Q: Which planet has the most obvious rings? | A: Saturn
Q: What is the name of Earth's moon? | A: The Moon
Q: Which planet is the largest? | A: Jupiter
Q: Which planet is known for its big storms? | A: Jupiter
Q: Which planet is famous for its tilted spin? | A: Uranus
Q: Which planet is farthest from the Sun? | A: Neptune
Q: What do we call a rocky object that orbits the Sun? | A: An asteroid
Q: What is a shooting star really called? | A: A meteor
Q: What do we call a planet that orbits a star outside our solar system? | A: An exoplanet
Q: What force keeps planets in orbit? | A: Gravity
Q: How long is one year on Earth? | A: About 365 days
Q: What is the name of the galaxy we live in? | A: The Milky Way
Q: What is the path a planet takes around the Sun called? | A: An orbit
Q: Which planet is known as Earth's twin? | A: Venus
Q: What do we call a huge cloud of gas and dust in space? | A: A nebula
Q: Which planet has a day longer than its year? | A: Venus
Q: What is the Sun mostly made of? | A: Hydrogen and helium
