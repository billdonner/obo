Title: Grammar & Punctuation

Q: What is a noun? | A: A person, place, thing, or idea
Q: What is a verb? | A: An action or state of being
Q: What is an adjective? | A: A word that describes a noun
Q: What is an adverb? | A: A word that describes a verb
Q: What is a pronoun? | A: A word that replaces a noun
Q: What is a conjunction? | A: A word that joins
Q: What is a preposition? | A: Shows relationship between words
Q: What is a subject? | A: Who or what the sentence is about
Q: What is a predicate? | A: What the subject does
Q: What is a complete sentence? | A: Has a subject and a verb
Q: What is a fragment? | A: An incomplete sentence
Q: What is a run-on sentence? | A: Two sentences stuck together
Q: What does a period do? | A: Ends a sentence
Q: What does a question mark do? | A: Ends a question
Q: What does an exclamation mark do? | A: Shows strong feeling
Q: What does a comma do? | A: Separates items or ideas
Q: What do quotation marks show? | A: Someone's exact words
Q: What does a colon introduce? | A: A list or explanation
Q: What is a homophone? | A: Words that sound the same
Q: What is a synonym? | A: A word with a similar meaning
