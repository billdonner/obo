Title: Earth Science

Q: What do we call melted rock under Earth's surface? | A: Magma
Q: What do we call melted rock that reaches the surface? | A: Lava
Q: What is the outermost layer of Earth called? | A: The crust
Q: What do we call a shaking of Earth's surface? | A: An earthquake
Q: What tool measures earthquakes? | A: A seismograph
Q: What is the process of breaking down rocks by wind or water? | A: Weathering
Q: What is the movement of soil and rock by water, wind, or ice? | A: Erosion
Q: What is the cycle of water called? | A: The water cycle
Q: What gas do plants take in? | A: Carbon dioxide
Q: What gas do plants release? | A: Oxygen
Q: What is the layer of air around Earth called? | A: The atmosphere
Q: What do we call frozen water in clouds? | A: Ice crystals
Q: What kind of rock forms from cooled lava? | A: Igneous
Q: What kind of rock forms from layers pressed together? | A: Sedimentary
Q: What kind of rock changes with heat and pressure? | A: Metamorphic
Q: What is the hottest layer of Earth called? | A: The core
Q: What is a large body of salt water called? | A: An ocean
Q: What is a large body of fresh water called? | A: A lake
Q: What do we call a place where a river meets the ocean? | A: An estuary
Q: What is a long, deep crack in the ocean floor called? | A: A trench
