Title: Animal Adaptations

Q: Why do polar bears have thick fur? | A: To stay warm
Q: Why do camels have humps? | A: To store fat
Q: Why do ducks have webbed feet? | A: For swimming
Q: Why do owls have large eyes? | A: To see in the dark
Q: Why do giraffes have long necks? | A: To reach tall leaves
Q: Why do porcupines have quills? | A: For protection
Q: Why do chameleons change color? | A: To blend in
Q: Why do kangaroos have strong legs? | A: For hopping
Q: Why do beavers have flat tails? | A: For swimming and balance
Q: Why do whales have blubber? | A: To stay warm
Q: Why do bats use echolocation? | A: To find food and avoid obstacles
Q: Why do birds have hollow bones? | A: To be lighter for flight
Q: Why do fish have gills? | A: To breathe underwater
Q: Why do snakes have scales? | A: To protect their skin
Q: Why do armadillos have armor? | A: To protect them
Q: Why do rabbits have big ears? | A: To hear predators
Q: Why do woodpeckers have strong beaks? | A: To drill into trees
Q: Why do penguins have flippers? | A: For swimming
Q: Why do frogs have sticky tongues? | A: To catch insects
Q: Why do turtles have shells? | A: For protection
