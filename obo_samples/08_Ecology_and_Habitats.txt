Title: Ecology & Habitats

Q: What do we call animals that eat only plants? | A: Herbivores
Q: What do we call animals that eat only meat? | A: Carnivores
Q: What do we call animals that eat both? | A: Omnivores
Q: What is a food chain? | A: Who eats whom in nature
Q: What do plants need to make food? | A: Sunlight, water, and CO2
Q: What do we call a place an animal lives? | A: A habitat
Q: What is a group of the same kind of animals called? | A: A population
Q: What is a community in ecology? | A: All living things in an area
Q: What is an ecosystem? | A: Living things and their environment
Q: What do decomposers do? | A: Break down dead things
Q: Name a decomposer. | A: Mushrooms or bacteria
Q: What is a producer? | A: An organism that makes its own food
Q: What is a consumer? | A: An organism that eats others
Q: What is migration? | A: Seasonal movement of animals
Q: What is hibernation? | A: Long winter sleep
Q: What is adaptation? | A: A trait that helps survival
Q: What is camouflage? | A: Blending in
Q: What is pollution? | A: Harmful substances in the environment
Q: What is recycling? | A: Turning old items into new
Q: Why are bees important? | A: They pollinate plants
