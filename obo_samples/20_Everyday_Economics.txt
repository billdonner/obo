Title: Everyday Economics

Q: What is money used for? | A: To buy goods and services
Q: What is a budget? | A: A plan for spending
Q: What does 'save' mean? | A: Keep money for later
Q: What does 'spend' mean? | A: Use money to buy
Q: What is a need? | A: Something you must have
Q: What is a want? | A: Something you would like
Q: What is a job? | A: Work people do to earn money
Q: What is income? | A: Money you earn
Q: What is a price? | A: How much something costs
Q: What is a bank? | A: A place to keep money
Q: What is a loan? | A: Money borrowed
Q: What is interest? | A: Extra money paid back
Q: What is a receipt? | A: Proof of purchase
Q: What is a bargain? | A: A good price
Q: What is a sale? | A: When items cost less
Q: What is a tax? | A: Money paid to the government
Q: What is a donation? | A: Money or items given to help
Q: What is a charity? | A: An organization that helps
Q: What is a consumer? | A: A person who buys
Q: What is a producer? | A: A person or company that makes goods
